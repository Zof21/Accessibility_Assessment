Golden Rules of Interface Design

  1. Strive for consistency
  2. Seek universal usability
  3. Offer informative feedback
  4. Design dialogs to yield closure
  5. Prevent errors
  6. Permit easy reversal of actions
  7. Keep users in control
  8. Reduce short-term memory load

1. Strive for consistency

  • Warsaw University of Technology

    * Menu stays at the same spot

    * 10 contrast errors

    * Always coming back to the home page by clicking on the logo

  • University of Wroclaw

    * Menu moves

    * 40 contrast errors

    * Always coming back to the home page by clicking on the logo

1. Strive for consistency

2. Seek universal usability

  • Warsaw University of Technology

    * No alt text for every image

    * 10 contrast errors - difficulties for people with poor eyesight

    * Not bad navigation without a mouse

    * Page in Polish and English

  • University of Wroclaw

    * No errors acording to WAVE

    * 40 contrast errors - difficulties for people with poor eyesight

    * Possibility of increasing the contrast (0 contrast errors)

    * Difficult to navigate without a mouse

    * Ability to enlarge the font

    * Page in Polish, English and German

2. Seek universal usability

3. Offer informative feedback

  • Warsaw University of Technology

    * Buttons change when hovered over or pressed

    * Search engine shows results or informs that there are no results

  • University of Wroclaw

    * Buttons change when hovered over or pressed

    * Search engine shows results or informs that there are no results

3. Offer informative feedback

4. Design dialogs to yield closure

  • Activities should be grouped and structured, so they should have a clearly communicated beginning, middle and end - this may apply at the university when enrolling in classes or registering to university. This cannot be checked without the account.

5. Prevent errors

  • Any digital product should inform the user about the error, its severity and how to fix it. This was checked on both websides when logging in and in both cases the messages were readable and indicated the place where the error was made.

5. Prevent errors

6. Permit easy reversal of actions

  • Warsaw University of Technology

    * Easy return to the home page

  • University of Wroclaw

    * Sometimes returning to the home page is not intuitive

7. Keep users in control

  • Warsaw University of Technology

    * Main menu containing pages frequently used by users is always visible, making it easy to switch between pages

  • University of Wroclaw

    * Main menu is not always visible

    * Additional information when hovering over the field

8. Reduce short-term memory load

  • Warsaw University of Technology

    * Displays what the user entered when searching

    * Website location remains visible

  • University of Wroclaw

    * Displays what the user entered when searching

    * Website location remains visible
